Profile

Built and scaled Denmark’s first fully online FGU school from concept to 50 active learners nationwide — as founding instructional designer, content lead, and one of two founding teachers. More than fifty students reached their goals across vocational education, HF and gymnasium, employment, and — for some — sustained daily structure as the precondition for any of those.

Designs and operates digital learning environments end-to-end: Moodle architecture, learning pathways, self-built H5P activities at scale, video and onboarding content, and the daily teaching practice that feeds the platform’s iteration. Works as the bindeled between pedagogy, technology, and organisation — translating technical requirements into teaching practice and pedagogical needs into platform decisions, rather than handing artefacts between specialists.

Continuous feedback loops between classroom and platform: learner response, completion patterns, and direct observation drive the next iteration. The build does not finish at launch; it merges into the running.

Academic background in cross-cultural studies, postcolonial and transnational literature, SLA/FLA theory, and film and media studies, applied directly to instructional design for diverse learner populations.
